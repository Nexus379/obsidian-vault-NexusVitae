# 🛒 Shopping Guide: Art & Craft Supplies

Here is the golden rule for buying art and craft supplies to get the best price-performance ratio without sacrificing quality.

## 1. Specialty Art Stores / Fine Art Supplies
**Used for:** Everything that needs to be precise, lightfast, and highly durable.
- **Watercolor Paper** (Only 100% Cotton, e.g., Arches)
- **Fine Colored Pencils** (e.g., Polychromos, Prismacolor)
- **Fine Brushes** (for details)
- **Professional Paints** (Schmincke, Winsor & Newton), if the painting is to be sold or hung for decades.

## 2. Supermarket Action Goods (Lidl, Aldi)
**Used for:** "White-label" goods in solid hobby/student quality at bargain prices.
- **Brands:** Crelando (Lidl), United Office (Lidl).
- **Canvases:** Absolutely unbeatable. Perfectly primed and sturdy.
- **Acrylic Paints:** Very good beginner quality, perfect for large areas and practice.
- **Modeling Sets:** Clay tools, wire loops, spatulas.
- **Printer Paper:** United Office is the absolute price-performance winner.

## 3. 1-Euro Discount Stores (Tedi, Woolworth, Action)
**Used for:** PURE CRAFTING MATERIALS (Crafting & Deco). 
> [!WARNING] Caution
> **Never** buy paints, brushes, canvases, or pencils here! The quality (chalk dust in paints, scratching leads, shedding brush hairs) ruins the joy of art.

What you can buy here **very well** and extremely cheaply:
- **Hot Glue Sticks:** Melt wonderfully, often available in large bulk packs.
- **Craft Felt & EVA Foam:** Same quality as in expensive craft stores.
- **Deco Elements:** Glitter, sequins, googly eyes, pipe cleaners, feathers.
- **Popsicle Sticks (Wooden Spatulas):** For mixing paints, resin, or for crafting.

## 🧹 Secret Household Cleaning Hacks for Brushes
Never buy expensive, toxic brush cleaners again. Use these cheap household alternatives based on the paint type:

### 1. Watercolors & Acrylics
* **The Absolute Champion:** Curd Soap or Soft Soap (*Kernseife / Schmierseife*). Lather the brush in lukewarm water with a simple, cheap bar of curd soap. It pulls out acrylics effortlessly and conditions the bristles so they don't get stiff.
* **For Dried/Crusty Acrylics:** Hand sanitizer or Isopropyl alcohol. The alcohol breaks down the hardened acrylic polymers, potentially saving a ruined brush!

### 2. Oil Paints
* **The Non-Toxic Hack:** Cheap Vegetable/Sunflower/Baby Oil & Dish Soap. You **DO NOT** need toxic, smelly Turpentine! Since oil paint is oil-based, it dissolves in other oils. Massage cooking oil into the bristles, wipe the paint out onto a paper towel, and repeat. Once clean, wash the remaining cooking oil out with regular dish soap (*Spülmittel*) and water. Your brushes are clean, soft, and your room doesn't smell like chemicals!
